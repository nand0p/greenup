Improving air quality By reducing harmful emissions and pollutants from industries, transportation, and other sources, GREENUP can significantly improve air quality. This benefits both human health and biodiversity by reducing respiratory issues, smog, and acid rain.
Protecting biodiversity Preserving natural habitats and ecosystems through conservation efforts helps protect endangered species and maintain biodiversity. This is essential for maintaining the balance of ecosystems and preventing the loss of valuable plant and animal species. Currently, 498 species considered vulnerable, and 111 critically, according to the Barcelona Zoo Foundation and UICN Red List.
Unused resources Barcelona rooftops are an unused potential resource which can easily be repurposed. The total rooftop area in Barcelona is estimated to be around 526,000 square meters.
Rainwater Absorbtion Absorbing rainwater also helps to reduce flooding and erosion and can help to improve the quality of local water bodies by reducing the amount of pollutants that are carried into rivers and lakes. By filtering out contaminants such as sediment, nutrients, and chemicals, rainwater absorption can help to protect water quality and habitat for aquatic life.
Mitigating Urban Heat Island Effect Green roofs have been proven to help reduce heat islands. Trees, green roofs, and vegetation can help reduce urban heat island effect by shading building surfaces, deflecting radiation from the sun, and releasing moisture into the atmosphere.
Mitigating Climate Change Implementing measures to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change has significant environmental benefits. This includes reducing the severity of natural disasters, preserving polar ice caps, and protecting vulnerable habitats from the impacts of global warming.



Catalunia Indigenous Drought Resistant Plant Species
Lavender (Lavandula angustifolia) A drought-tolerant plant known for its fragrant purple flowers. It requires well-drained soil and full sun.
Rosemary (Rosmarinus officinalis) Another aromatic herb that thrives in arid conditions. It prefers dry, rocky soil and full sun.
Spanish broom (Spartium junceum) A shrub with bright yellow flowers that can tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
Prickly pear cactus (Opuntia spp.) A succulent plant that stores water in its fleshy pads, making it well-suited for arid landscapes.
Agave (Agave spp.) A striking plant with rosettes of thick, spiky leaves that excel in hot, dry climates.
Lavender cotton (Santolina chamaecyparissus) A compact shrub with silver-gray foliage and yellow button-like flowers that thrives in well-drained, dry soil.
Spanish lavender (Lavandula stoechas) A drought-tolerant lavender variety that produces showy purple flowers with distinctive "rabbit ear" bracts.
Montpellier Cistus (Cistus monspeliensis) A flowering shrub that is native to Catalonia and is commonly found in the region's dry and rocky areas.
 

Larger Catalunia Indigenous Plant and Tree Species
Aleppo Pine (Pinus halepensis) A common tree species found throughout Catalonia, particularly in the coastal areas.
Stone Pine (Pinus pinea) A pine tree species found in Catalonia's coastal areas, known for its edible pine nuts.
Holm Oak (Quercus ilex) Native Oak species found in Catalonia's forests and woodlands.
Cork Oak (Quercus suber) Native Oak species that is native to Catalonia and is known for its valuable cork bark.


Overall, environmental benefits help create a healthier and more sustainable planet for current and future generations. It is essential to continue implementing and supporting initiatives that promote environmental protection and sustainability.
